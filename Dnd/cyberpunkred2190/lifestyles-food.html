01 THE FOOD CHAIN FOOD

In 2190, real food is a luxury. The global ecosystem never recovered from the ecological collapse of the mid-21st century. Most of the world eats synthetic protein, processed algae, and lab-grown substitutes. Actual meat, vegetables, and fruit are grown in Biotechnica's sealed agricultural domes and sold at prices that would make a pre-war grocery shopper weep.

Night City's food economy breaks down into five tiers, and what tier you eat at says everything about who you are:

Food Tiers

Gutter: Kibble, scavenged rations, dumpster finds. You eat to not die.

Street: Vendor carts, noodle stands, vending machines. Cheap, hot, and it'll keep you moving.

Working: Sit-down diners, chain restaurants, decent synth-meat. A normal meal for a normal person.

Corpo: Executive cafeterias, upscale restaurants, imported synth-premium. You eat well because you can afford to.

Luxury: Real food. Biotechnica organic. Embers-tier dining. The kind of meal most people in Night City will never taste in their entire lives.

05 REAL FOOD — BIOTECHNICA & ORGANIC GOODS ORGANIC

"Real food is the last honest luxury. You can fake chrome, fake a smile, fake an identity. You can't fake a tomato."

Biotechnica controls the majority of real food production through sealed agricultural domes and orbital greenhouses. Organic goods are sold through licensed retailers at prices that reflect their scarcity. Buying real food on the black market is possible but risky — counterfeits are common.

07 LIFESTYLE COSTS & HOUSING LIFESTYLE

Where you sleep and how you live costs money every month. Lifestyle represents your ongoing expenses: rent, utilities, food, basic maintenance, and the general cost of existing in Night City without dying of exposure or malnutrition.

LifestyleDescriptionMonthly Cost
Homeless / SquattingGUTTER You sleep where you can. Alleys, underpasses, abandoned buildings, coffin stacks if someone leaves one unlocked. No address. No security. No guarantees you'll wake up with all your organs. 0eb
Cube / Coffin HotelGUTTER A 2m x 1m sleeping pod with a lock, a vent, and a shared bathroom down the hall. You rent by the night. It's not living. It's storage. 500eb
Cargo Container / Converted SpaceSTREET A repurposed shipping container, garage, or utility room. You've got walls, a door that locks, and maybe running water. Congratulations: you have an address. 1,000eb
Megabuilding Apartment (Small)STREET A single room in a megabuilding. Shared hallway, thin walls, noisy neighbors, but it's a real apartment. Comes with basic utilities and a communal laundry on your floor. 2,000eb
Apartment (Standard)WORKING A proper apartment. Bedroom, kitchen, bathroom. Maybe a window that faces something other than another wall. This is what "making it" looks like for most Night City residents. 5,000eb
Apartment (Upscale)CORPO Two bedrooms, a real kitchen, building security, and a view. Climate-controlled, soundproofed, and your neighbors don't cook meth. You're doing well. 15,000eb
Corporate HousingCORPO Company-provided residence in a secured corpo zone. High-quality, heavily monitored. Free as long as you're employed. The moment you're not, you have 48 hours to vacate. Free (employed) / N/A
Penthouse / Private ResidenceLUXURY Top-floor living with panoramic views, private security, dedicated parking, and enough space to feel like the city can't touch you. The kind of place where the elevator requires biometric access and the fridge has real food in it. 50,000–200,000eb
Lifestyle & Downtime: A character's lifestyle determines their baseline comfort during downtime activities. Programming quickhacks in a coffin hotel is technically possible but miserable. The DM may rule that certain downtime activities (therapy, long rest recovery, crafting) require at least Working-tier housing to be effective.
Lifestyle costs are a great way to drain eddies between runs and keep the economy feeling real. If the party is swimming in cash, remind them that rent is due. If they're broke, let them feel the weight of eating kibble for a week while the Fixer's contact dines at Embers. The gap between tiers isn't just mechanical — it's narrative.
