01 HUMANITY & EMPATHY — CORE RULES HUMANITY

"Chrome don't make you less human. Losing yourself in it does."

Humanity is a pool that represents a character's connection to their emotions, relationships, and sanity. Every piece of cyberware, every traumatic event, and every line crossed chips away at it. When it's gone, so are you.

Core Concepts

Humanity (HUM): A numerical pool representing your emotional and psychological stability.

Empathy (EMP): Derived stat, same as Cyberpunk RED. Every 10 Humanity = 1 EMP.

Cyberpsychosis Threshold: When Humanity reaches 0, the character is lost. The DM decides what happens to them.

02 STARTING HUMANITY BY CLASS HUMANITY

Instead of Cyberpunk RED's role-specific EMP values, ATR maps starting Humanity to 5e class archetypes. This keeps the RED flavor: social and cerebral characters tend to hold onto more humanity, while combat operatives who are often already implanted before play begins start with less to spare.

Archetype5e ClassesStarting HumanityAvg. HUMEMP Range
Solos / Frontliners Fighters, Barbarians, Monks, Paladins 6d6 ~21 1–3
Support / Specialists Rogues, Rangers, Artificers, Clerics (non-Knowledge) 6d8 ~27 1–4
Brains / Netrunners Wizards, Warlocks, Sorcerers, Bards, Knowledge Clerics 6d10 ~33 1–6
Frontliners tend to already have implants before the campaign starts, meaning their lower pool is partially accounted for. A Solo who's been in the field for years and already has a Sandevistan and Subdermal Armor is starting at a realistic place. A freshly chromed Wizard hasn't been through that grinder yet.

03 HUMANITY LOSS HUMANITY

From Cyberware

Each implant has a Humanity Cost — either a flat value or a dice roll. Refer to the cyberware entries in the RED Gear Catalog and Homebrew Cyberware Compendium for specific HL values per item.

From Trauma & Stress

The DM can call for Humanity loss in response to severe psychological events. These are usually small (1–3 points) but can stack over time.

Trauma Examples

1 HL: Witnessing a particularly brutal death, being forced to harm an innocent, minor betrayal.

2 HL: Torture (giving or receiving), killing someone you know, a close call with cyberpsychosis.

3 HL: Death of a loved one, major personal betrayal, committing an atrocity.

From Street Drugs

Several combat drugs inflict Humanity Loss as part of their effects. See the Street Drugs section below for specifics.

Cumulative Burnout: Once your EMP drops a tier (e.g., from EMP 3 to EMP 2), all Humanity in that tier is gone for good — unless recovered through roleplay, therapy, or life goals. You can't buy it back with eddies.

04 CYBERPSYCHOSIS & SAVING THROWS HUMANITY

When a character's Humanity is under pressure — taking a big HL hit, witnessing something horrifying while already low, or being pushed past their limits — the DM can call for a Cyberpsychosis Saving Throw.

Saving Throw Ability: Use the ability score most tied to the character's coping mechanism:

WIS — Meditation, faith, discipline. The monk who centers themselves.
CHA — Music, performance, social bonding. The rockerboy who plays through the pain.
INT — Rationalization, technical tinkering, problem-solving. The netrunner who thinks their way through it.
CON — Pure grit, endurance, "push it down and keep moving." The solo who just doesn't stop.

Failure Consequences

Failing a Cyberpsychosis save doesn't immediately mean full psychosis. It triggers episodes proportional to how low your Humanity is:

EMP 3+: Momentary detachment. Cold response to something that should matter. A flicker.

EMP 2: Paranoia, sudden violent impulses, brief hallucinations. You snap back, but it takes a second.

EMP 1: Full episodes — intrusive thoughts, sustained paranoia, hallucinations, sudden violence. The DM can introduce these unprompted.

EMP 0: Cyberpsychotic Break. The character is no longer playable.

At the DM's discretion, a character at EMP 0 could become a temporary enemy combatant for one final, memorable scene rather than simply being removed. Your table, your call.

05 HUMANITY RECOVERY HUMANITY

Humanity isn't a one-way street. Characters can claw their way back from the edge — but it takes effort, time, and genuine connection. You can't shortcut this with chrome or eddies.

Positive Relationships: Deep friendships, romances, or meaningful trust-building moments. +1d4 Humanity at the DM's discretion. This can't be farmed — it has to feel earned.
Life Goals Achieved: Completing something central to your character's identity — a personal quest, a promise fulfilled, a long-term objective reached. +1–2 EMP (that's 10–20 Humanity).
Therapy / Long Downtime: A full rest season with a therapist, mentor, or safe community. Requires actual downtime in a safe environment — not a weekend at a coffin hotel. Regain 1d6 Humanity.
Major Losses: The death of a loved one, betrayal by someone in your core bonds, or the destruction of something you built your identity around causes an immediate −1 EMP (10 Humanity), no save. Think David Martinez going over the edge when he thought Lucy was dead.

06 ROLEPLAY AT LOW HUMANITY HUMANITY

Humanity isn't just a number — it should change how a character feels at the table.

EMP LevelBehavioral Profile
EMP 4+Empathetic, social, capable of strong bonds. Feels like a person.
EMP 3Functional but emotionally blunted. Relationships feel more transactional. Harder to connect.
EMP 2Detached, cold. Violent impulses surface. Paranoia creeps in. Others notice you're "off."
EMP 1Teetering on the edge. DM introduces intrusive thoughts, paranoia, hallucinations. Every chrome installation is a gamble.

07 STREET DRUGS STREET DRUGS

"Stims? Combat drugs? Something to make a Friday Night Firefight a little more fun? I've got what you need." — Hornet, Fixer of Heywood

Street Drugs have a Primary Effect (automatic) and a Secondary Effect that triggers on a failed check (DV listed per drug). All ATR prices are ×10 from RED source values.

BERSERKER

ATR PRICE: 1,000eb per dose (RED: 100eb) • SECONDARY DV: 17
"I've seen a Solo on Berserker shrug off a rifle shot that blew off her leg, and proceed to strangle the shooter to death with her bare hands."
PRIMARY EFFECT (10 Minutes)
  • Critical Injuries deal no Bonus Damage.
  • Penalties from Seriously/Mortally Wounded states are halved.
  • Penalties from losing a Facedown are halved.
  • User becomes highly aggressive. Roleplay accordingly.
SECONDARY EFFECT (DV17 FAIL)
  • 2 points of Humanity Loss.
  • Addiction: Base Death Save Penalty permanently increased by 1.

PRIME TIME

ATR PRICE: 500eb per dose (RED: 50eb) • SECONDARY DV: 17
"Used to make you a cold, calculated killer on the battlefield. Now it makes you a cold, calculated killer in the office."
PRIMARY EFFECT (4 Hours)
  • Take 4d6 Humanity Loss on use (returned after the effect ends). Cannot reduce below 0 HUM.
  • COOL and WILL increased by 2 (WILL increase does not affect HP).
  • Emotions are repressed. Roleplay accordingly.
SECONDARY EFFECT (DV17 FAIL)
  • 1 point of permanent Humanity Loss.
  • Addiction: COOL lowered by 2 unless currently on Prime Time.

SIXGUN

ATR PRICE: 1,000eb per dose (RED: 100eb) • SECONDARY DV: 17
"When you need someone to dive into a palace of military-grade Black ICE and burn a paper trail, you gotta give them the best tools."
PRIMARY EFFECT (4 Hours)
  • MOVE and REF reduced by 2 (minimum 1).
  • +2 to Speed while Jacked In.
  • Unsafe Jack Outs (not caused by programs) are treated as Safe.
  • Once per turn: spend 1 Humanity to gain 1 additional NET Action.
SECONDARY EFFECT (DV17 FAIL)
  • Addiction: −2 to Speed while Jacked In unless currently on Sixgun.

SECONDARY EFFECT (DV17 FAIL)

PIRANHA SMASH

ATR PRICE: 100eb per dose (RED: 10eb) • SECONDARY DV: 9
"Like regular Smash but with a twist of lime and 40% less chance of addiction!"
PRIMARY EFFECT (4 Hours)

Healing Pharma — Airhypo Cartridges

"In 5e, they're potions. In Night City, they're pressurized cartridges loaded into an Airhypo injector. Same result: you stop bleeding. The expensive ones just do it faster."

Healing in ATR is handled through Airhypo cartridges — single-use pharmaceutical loads delivered via a pneumatic injector pressed against the skin. No needles, no prep, no waiting. Just a hiss and you're patched. Each cartridge type corresponds to a traditional 5e healing potion tier.

Airhypo Usage Rules

Action Cost: Using an Airhypo cartridge on yourself or an adjacent creature costs a Bonus Action.

Delivery: Requires an Airhypo device (500eb, see below). The device is reusable — only the cartridge is consumed.

Stacking: You can use multiple cartridges in one encounter, but using more than 2 within 1 minute causes nausea — disadvantage on all checks until the end of your next turn.

Stims vs Street Drugs: Combat stims are single-use pharmaceuticals with no addiction mechanic — they're too expensive and short-lasting to abuse regularly. Street Drugs (Berserker, Prime Time, etc.) have addiction saves and ongoing side effects. The two systems are separate. You CAN use a stim and a street drug at the same time, but the DM may rule that combining them has unpredictable side effects.

10 DRUG-RELATED CYBERWARE CYBERWARE

BODYWEIGHT AUTOINJECTOR

ATR PRICE: 1,000eb (RED: 100eb) • HL: 3 (1d6) • INSTALL: Clinic • TYPE: Internal Body Cyberware
"No need to stop to shoot up when the shooting starts, and no one's gonna clock an autoinjector in a pat down."
  • Load one dose of a Street Drug into a subdermal reservoir (Action).
  • Inject as an Action on command.
  • With a Biomonitor: can auto-inject when rolling Initiative, receiving a Critical Injury, or entering a chosen Wound State.

PURSUIT SECURITY INC. GAS JET

ATR PRICE: 5,000eb (RED: 500eb) • HL: 7 (2d6) • INSTALL: Clinic • TYPE: Cyberarm Option (2 slots)
"Load an experimental toxin, blast a crowd of rentacops, and just watch. Perfect sample size!"
  • Aerosol gas launcher loaded with 3 doses of a Street Drug, or 3 vials of Poison/Biotoxin.
  • Concealable without a check.
  • Mechanically acts as a One-Handed Exotic Shotgun (Shotgun Shell spread mode only).
  • Instead of damage, applies loaded substance effects to all vulnerable targets in the spread.
  • Nasal Filters and similar gas-nullifying items negate the attack.
  • Each shot fully drains the weapon. Requires full reload.
