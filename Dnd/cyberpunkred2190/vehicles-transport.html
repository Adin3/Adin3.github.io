01 PUBLIC TRANSPORT TRANSPORT

"The NCART runs on time. Delamain runs on money. And if you're hitchhiking in the Badlands, you're running on prayers."

Not everyone in Night City owns a car — and frankly, not everyone should. Between parking in a megabuilding (if you can find a spot that isn't already claimed by a gang) and the risk of getting your ride bombed, jacked, or impounded, public transit is the sensible option for anyone who isn't running hot.

02 VEHICLE STATS — READING THE SHEET RULES

Vehicles in ATR use the 5e Vehicle stat block format (compatible with Foundry VTT vehicle sheets). Here's what each stat means:

Vehicle Stat Block

Size: Large (cars, bikes), Huge (trucks, vans), or Gargantuan (AVs, tanks).

AC: How hard the vehicle is to damage. Represents hull integrity and armor plating.

HP: Total hit points before the vehicle is destroyed or disabled.

Speed: Maximum speed in feet per round. Divide by roughly 10 for a mph approximation during chases.

STR / DEX: Used for ramming (STR) and handling (DEX). Most vehicles have 0 for mental stats.

Damage Immunities: All vehicles are immune to Poison and Psychic damage.

Equipment — Tires: Each tire has its own AC and HP. Destroying a tire imposes penalties (see Driving Rules).

Equipment — Weapons: Mounted weapons, if any. Listed with their own attack bonus and damage.

Crew / Passengers: How many people the vehicle can carry, including the driver.

Targeting Components: Attackers can target the vehicle as a whole (attack vs vehicle AC) or specific components (tires, weapons, engine). Targeting a specific component is a called shot — attack roll is made at disadvantage but hits the component directly.

06 DRIVING RULES RULES

Driving in Night City uses DEX (Vehicle Handling) checks. Proficiency with Vehicles (Land) or Vehicles (Air) applies if the character has it.

Driver Actions (Per Turn)

On their turn, the driver chooses one of the following as their Action:

Speed: Focus on going fast. The vehicle moves at full speed. No attacks, no fancy moves. The driver's full attention is on the road.

Maneuver: Focus on handling. The vehicle moves at half speed, but the driver can make sharp turns, weave through traffic, or avoid obstacles (DEX Vehicle Handling check, DC set by DM). On success, the maneuver is executed cleanly.

Reckless Action: The driver attempts to do something else while driving — fire a weapon, cast a spell, use an item. The vehicle continues at half speed. The driver makes their attack/action at disadvantage, and must succeed on a DC 13 DEX (Vehicle Handling) check or the vehicle veers off course (DM determines consequences: sideswipe, curb hit, spin out).

Passenger Actions

Passengers can act normally on their turns — attack, cast spells, use items. However:

• Ranged attacks from a moving vehicle are made at disadvantage unless the vehicle used the Speed action (straight line, stable platform).

• Melee attacks from a vehicle can only target adjacent vehicles, creatures within 5 ft, or targets during a pass-by (DM discretion).

• Spellcasting works normally, but Concentration saves may be required if the vehicle takes damage or executes a sharp maneuver.

Tire Damage

Tires can be targeted with a called shot (attack at disadvantage, targets Tire AC 15). Destroying tires has cumulative effects:

1 tire destroyed: Speed reduced by 25%. Disadvantage on Maneuver checks.

2 tires destroyed: Speed reduced by 50%. All driving checks are at disadvantage.

3+ tires destroyed: Vehicle cannot exceed half speed. Driver must make a DC 15 DEX check each round or lose control.

Run-Flat Tires: Ignore the first tire destruction. The second counts as the first. Expensive, but worth it.

Cover from Vehicles

Car (standard): Half cover (+2 AC, +2 DEX saves) for occupants.

Armored vehicle: Three-quarters cover (+5 AC, +5 DEX saves) for occupants.

Motorcycle: No cover. You're exposed.

Convertible / open top: Half cover from the sides, no cover from above.

07 VEHICLE COMBAT & CHASES COMBAT

"You haven't lived until you've fired a shotgun out the window of a Thorton doing 120 while someone's trying to ram you off the overpass."

Chase Mechanics

Chases use a simple Distance Track system. The DM sets an initial distance between the vehicles (Close, Medium, Far) and each round the drivers make opposed checks to change it.

Distance Bands

Close (0–60 ft): Vehicles can ram, sideswipe, and melee. Ranged attacks are normal (no disadvantage from distance). Passengers can attempt to jump between vehicles (DC 15 Athletics/Acrobatics).

Medium (60–300 ft): Standard ranged attack range. No melee. Ramming not possible without closing distance first.

Far (300+ ft): Long-range attacks only. Disadvantage on all ranged attacks. One more round at Far range and the pursued vehicle escapes (or the pursuer catches up, depending on who's faster).

Changing Distance

Each round, both drivers make an opposed DEX (Vehicle Handling) check. The winner shifts the distance one band in their favor (closer if pursuing, farther if fleeing). Ties maintain current distance.

Speed Advantage: If one vehicle's Speed stat is more than 200 ft higher than the other, that driver gets advantage on the opposed check.

Chases should be fast, chaotic, and cinematic. Don't track every foot of movement — use the Distance Bands to keep the narrative moving and let the players make dramatic choices rather than doing math. The best chases end with someone going through a billboard or off a bridge, not with a spreadsheet.

09 OWNERSHIP, REGISTRATION & INSURANCE RULES

"You think the DMV is bad in the real world? In Night City, the line is three hours long, the clerk has a shotgun under the counter, and your registration fee just went up because you looked at them wrong."

Owning a vehicle in Night City involves paperwork. Yes, even in 2190. The NCPD and Night City Department of Motor Vehicles (NCDMV) technically require all vehicles to be registered. In practice, enforcement depends on which district you're in and how much the local cops care.

Vehicle ownership is a great ongoing expense that drains eddies between runs. Registration fees, insurance, parking, fuel, repairs — it all adds up. A party that buys a 50,000eb muscle car and then can't afford to insure it or park it is going to learn a hard lesson about Night City's economy. Also, the Mercenary Vehicle License is intentionally funny in-world — it's basically the city admitting that mercs exist and need to mount guns on things, so they might as well get a cut.
